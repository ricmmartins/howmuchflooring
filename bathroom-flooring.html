Best Bathroom Flooring Options

🏺 Porcelain Tile

The most popular bathroom flooring choice. Water absorption <0.5%. Extremely durable and available in countless styles.

  • Cost: $3–$8/sqft material
  • Pros: Extremely durable, waterproof, high resale value
  • Cons: Cold underfoot (pair with heated floor mat), harder DIY install

🪵 Luxury Vinyl Plank (LVP)

100% waterproof and increasingly popular for bathrooms. Warm underfoot and easy to install as a DIY project.

  • Cost: $2–$5/sqft material
  • Pros: Waterproof, warm, easy DIY, looks like wood/stone
  • Cons: Can dent from heavy objects, shorter lifespan than tile

🔲 Ceramic Tile

Budget-friendly and waterproof when properly grouted. Available in a wide variety of colors, patterns, and sizes.

  • Cost: $1.50–$5/sqft material
  • Pros: Affordable, waterproof, huge style variety
  • Cons: Less dense than porcelain, can chip more easily

🪨 Natural Stone

Premium look with marble, slate, or travertine. Requires regular sealing to maintain water resistance.

  • Cost: $5–$15/sqft material
  • Pros: Stunning aesthetics, unique patterns, adds home value
  • Cons: Slippery when wet (choose honed finish), requires sealing, expensive

Bathroom Flooring Considerations

💧 Moisture Protection

Proper moisture management is critical in bathrooms. Always use a waterproof membrane beneath tile floors, especially in shower areas.

  • Substrate: Cement board (not drywall) for wet areas
  • Membrane: Liquid or sheet waterproof barrier under tile
  • Grout: Epoxy grout resists water better than cement grout

📏 Average Bathroom Sizes

  • Half bath: 18–30 sq ft
  • Full bath: 40–60 sq ft
  • Master bath: 60–100 sq ft

Use our calculator above to get an exact estimate for your bathroom dimensions.

🔥 Heated Floors

Radiant heating turns cold bathroom floors into a luxury feature. Best paired with tile flooring.

  • Electric mat: $5–$12/sqft installed
  • Radiant hydronic: $6–$16/sqft installed
  • Operating cost: $0.10–$0.25/day

🪄 Small Bathroom Tips

  • Large-format tile (12×24 or bigger) makes rooms look bigger
  • Minimal grout lines create a seamless appearance
  • Light colors open up the space visually
  • Diagonal tile layout adds perceived width
  • Continuous flooring from bath into hallway extends sightlines

Bathroom Flooring FAQ

What is the most waterproof bathroom flooring?
Porcelain tile and luxury vinyl plank (LVP/SPC) are the most waterproof options. Porcelain tile has <0.5% water absorption. SPC vinyl has a stone core that's 100% waterproof. Both are excellent for bathrooms.
Can I use hardwood flooring in a bathroom?
Not recommended for full bathrooms. Solid hardwood warps with moisture exposure. Engineered hardwood with waterproof finish may work in half baths with careful maintenance. Vinyl plank that looks like wood is a safer alternative.
How much does it cost to floor a bathroom?
Average bathroom (50 sq ft): Ceramic tile $200–$400 material, Vinyl plank $150–$300, Porcelain $200–$500. Installation adds $200–$600. Total: $400–$1,100 for an average bathroom.
Is heated flooring worth it in bathrooms?
Yes, especially in cold climates. Electric mat systems cost $5–$12/sqft and add $0.10–$0.25/day to operate. They pair best with tile flooring and add resale value.
What flooring is best for a small bathroom?
Large-format porcelain tile (12×24 or larger) with minimal grout lines makes small bathrooms appear larger. Light colors open up the space. Vinyl plank in a light wood tone is another excellent option.
