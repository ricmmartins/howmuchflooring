📏 Quick Size Reference

A 12×12 room is 144 sq ft (16 sq yards), common for bedrooms and home offices. Other names: 12-by-12, twelve by twelve.

Metric: approximately 3.66m × 3.66m = 13.4 sq meters.

📏 How to Measure Accurately

  • Measure at baseboard level
  • Measure twice
  • Include closet floors
  • Round up to nearest inch
  • For oddly shaped rooms, split into rectangles
  • Note door openings and transitions

💰 Money-Saving Tips

  • Buy during holiday sales (Memorial Day, Black Friday)
  • Consider remnants for small rooms
  • DIY installation saves $2–$8/sqft
  • Compare big-box stores vs. specialty shops
  • Buy 10% extra from same dye lot

Frequently Asked Questions

How much flooring do I need for a 12×12 room?
You need 144 sq ft of flooring. Add 10% for waste (standard recommendation), bringing the total to 159 sq ft. For diagonal or complex patterns, add 15–20% instead.
How much does it cost to floor a 12×12 room?
Material costs range from $240 for budget carpet to $1,270 for mid-range hardwood. Including installation: $400 (carpet) to $2,700 (premium hardwood). Vinyl plank is the best value at $636–$1,590 total.
How many tiles do I need for a 12×12 room?
For 12×12 inch tiles: 159 tiles. For 18×18 tiles: 71 tiles. For 24×24 tiles: 40 tiles. (All figures include 10% waste factor.)
How many boxes of laminate for a 12×12 room?
Assuming 24 sq ft per box: 159 ÷ 24 = 7 boxes (rounded up). Always check the coverage listed on your specific product — it varies by brand.
Is a 12×12 room big enough for a bedroom?
Yes, 144 sq ft is a standard bedroom size. It comfortably fits a queen bed with nightstands and a dresser. Average US bedrooms range from 100–200 sq ft.
