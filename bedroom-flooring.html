Best Bedroom Flooring Options

🥇 Carpet

#1 choice for bedrooms — $1–$4/sq ft

  • Warmest and softest underfoot — unbeatable comfort
  • Excellent noise reduction for peaceful sleep
  • Wide range of colors, textures, and pile heights
  • Ideal for master bedrooms and kids' rooms
  • Add quality padding for a luxury feel

🪵 Hardwood

Timeless elegance — $4–$12/sq ft

  • Adds significant resale value to your home
  • Use area rugs for added warmth and comfort
  • Best for master bedrooms and upscale spaces
  • Oak and maple are the most popular species
  • Easy to clean — great for allergy sufferers

💰 Laminate

Affordable wood look — $1.50–$4/sq ft

  • Easy maintenance and allergen-friendly surface
  • Good for kids' and guest rooms on a budget
  • Use underlayment for sound absorption
  • Click-lock installation is quick and DIY-friendly
  • Convincing wood-grain aesthetics

💎 Luxury Vinyl Plank

Versatile choice — $2–$5/sq ft

  • Water-resistant — great for kids' rooms and pet owners
  • Comfortable and quiet with proper underlayment
  • Available in realistic wood and stone looks
  • Durable and scratch-resistant surface
  • Easy click-lock DIY installation

Bedroom Flooring Considerations

📐 Average Bedroom Sizes

  • Small / Guest bedroom: 100–150 sq ft
  • Standard bedroom: 130–180 sq ft
  • Master bedroom: 200–350 sq ft
  • Kids' bedroom: 100–150 sq ft

🔇 Noise & Comfort

  • Noise: Carpet is the quietest option
  • Add underlayment to hard floors for 2nd-story bedrooms
  • Comfort ranking: Carpet > Vinyl > Laminate > Hardwood
  • Area rugs on hard floors provide warmth without sacrificing easy cleaning

🤧 Allergies & Resale Value

  • Allergies: Hard floors (hardwood, laminate, vinyl) are better for allergy sufferers
  • Carpet traps dust, pollen, and pet dander
  • Resale value: Hardwood > Carpet > Vinyl > Laminate
  • Updated flooring of any type helps homes sell faster

Bedroom Flooring FAQ

Is carpet or hardwood better for bedrooms?
Carpet is warmer, softer, and quieter — ideal for comfort. Hardwood is easier to clean, more durable, and better for resale value and allergy sufferers. Many homeowners choose hardwood with a plush area rug for the best of both worlds.
What's the best flooring for a kids' bedroom?
Carpet is the classic choice (soft for play, quiet, warm). However, vinyl plank is gaining popularity — it's waterproof, scratch-resistant, and easy to clean. For older kids, laminate is a budget-friendly option.
How much does it cost to floor a bedroom?
Standard bedroom (150 sq ft): Carpet $200–$750 (with padding/install), Laminate $350–$800, Vinyl plank $400–$900, Hardwood $800–$2,000. Carpet is the most budget-friendly option.
Does bedroom flooring affect home resale value?
Yes. Hardwood floors can increase home value by 2–5%. Carpet is expected in bedrooms by most buyers but doesn't add value. Luxury vinyl is neutral to positive. Updated flooring of any type helps sell faster.
How do I reduce noise with hard flooring in bedrooms?
Use premium underlayment (cork or felt, not foam). Add area rugs with thick padding. For upstairs bedrooms, acoustic underlayment rated STC 65+ is recommended. Engineered hardwood is quieter than laminate.
