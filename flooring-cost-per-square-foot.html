Regional Cost Variations

Northeast (NYC, Boston)

10–20% above national average. Higher labor costs drive up installation prices significantly. Example: hardwood installation $6–$14/sqft.

Southeast (Atlanta, Miami)

At or slightly below national average. Example: hardwood installation $4–$10/sqft. Hurricane-prone areas prefer tile and vinyl for moisture resistance.

Midwest (Chicago, Minneapolis)

Near national average. Carpet is popular for warmth during harsh winters. Example: hardwood installation $4–$11/sqft.

West Coast (LA, Seattle)

10–25% above national average in major cities. Example: hardwood installation $5–$13/sqft. Eco-friendly options like bamboo and cork are popular.

How to Save on Flooring Costs

  • Buy during sales events (Memorial Day, Black Friday, end-of-year clearance)
  • Consider DIY for click-lock floors (laminate, LVP) — saves $2–$6/sqft in labor
  • Get 3+ contractor quotes — prices vary significantly
  • Buy all material from the same dye lot to ensure color consistency
  • Consider slightly lower-grade materials for closets and low-traffic areas

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest flooring per square foot?
Sheet vinyl is the cheapest at $0.50–$2/sqft for materials. Including installation, carpet and sheet vinyl are most affordable at $2–$6/sqft total.
What is the most expensive flooring?
Natural stone (marble, granite) ranges from $12–$29/sqft installed. Exotic hardwoods (Brazilian walnut, teak) can exceed $30/sqft installed. Custom tile work with intricate patterns also runs high.
How much does it cost to floor a 1,000 sq ft house?
Budget (carpet/laminate): $3,500–$10,000. Mid-range (LVP/engineered hardwood): $6,000–$18,000. Premium (solid hardwood/tile): $8,000–$24,000. All include installation.
Does flooring cost include installation?
Prices listed per square foot usually mean material only unless specified. Always ask. Installation adds $1–$14/sqft depending on material type and your location. Get written quotes that itemize materials and labor.
What flooring has the best cost-to-value ratio?
Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) offers the best value: $4–$11/sqft installed with 15–25 year lifespan, waterproof, and realistic appearance. For maximum ROI, hardwood adds the most resale value.
